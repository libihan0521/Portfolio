There are 3 types of emotion journal tools in the current market: physical products, digital applications, and the indirect type involving some social interactions.

Physical Products

  • Card games or notepads specifically designed to help children recognize and express their emotions.
  • Notebooks featuring specially designed templates for recording daily emotions.

Digital Applications

These apps go beyond basic mood logging and visualization, incorporating clinical intervention techniques like CBT, attention-shifting, forward-looking strategies, and positive self-talk to enhance the user experience.

The indirect type involving some social interactions

  • An empathetic therapist who encourages emotional expression.
  • Share your feelings with a trusted person who provides a safe space for open communication.
  • Share emotions on social media for connection and support.

Insights from user research

Which individuals are more inclined to be attracted to this product?

Which groups might require some effort to win them over?

Feedback delay is the top reason why users quit an emotion journal product.

The primary positive feedback received in emotion tracking is when users revisit their old journals, triggering nostalgic memories or discovering patterns within their emotions.

However, the time required to accumulate a sufficient number of journals to attain such positive feedback is often lengthy. Additionally, the process of journaling itself can be draining for many individuals. If they miss a week of recording, it often leads to a longer gap of a month, eventually resulting in abandonment. Thus, establishing the habit of consistent journaling becomes a significant challenge. The primary reason users discontinue using an emotion journal product is due to the delay in receiving feedback.

Factors that contribute to users' continued engagement:

  • Elegant and visually appealing design: The interface is designed with a stylish and attractive aesthetic, captivating users with its visual appeal.
  • User-friendly logging features: The logging features are designed to provide a comfortable and seamless experience, enabling users to record rich and meaningful content concisely and effortlessly.
  • Comprehensive statistics and analysis: The inclusion of in-depth statistics empowers users to gain insights and analyze patterns within their emotional data, facilitating a deeper understanding of their emotional well-being.
  • Engaging and supportive communities: The presence of active and supportive communities fosters a sense of belonging and encourages users to connect, share experiences, and receive valuable support from like-minded individuals.

Persona

Primary persona

Based on the research findings, I have chosen young individuals, approximately 14 to 25 years old, as my primary target audience. These individuals have little to no experience with emotion-tracking software, lack social-emotional learning, and may be facing emotional challenges. My decision to focus on this group is not solely driven by rational analysis or profit-seeking motives. Instead, it stems from a genuine desire to empower them. I want to provide them with a tool that offers solace in their emotions and helps establish a strong psychological foundation for their future. I aim to share the transformative experience I had when I began caring about my own emotions. Although their initial attraction may be the game's entertainment value, they will ultimately experience personal growth through the engaging elements.


Secondary persona

The secondary personas consist of individuals who are already using or have used emotion-tracking software but are seeking a superior tool. These secondary users can be further categorized based on their emotional tracking goals:

1. Those who rely on such products to maintain their mental well-being.

2. Those who use them for personal growth and memory preservation.

You may have noticed that all three personas are Chinese. This is because I wanted to have a specific cultural background to guide the creation of game content. Additionally, as someone with firsthand experience of the emotional suppression and neglect in Chinese society as an East Asian community, I want to chose China as the cultural backdrop of this project.

Reaching the final design of "Bunny Mood" wasn't an immediate process. Honestly, I even didn't reach a clear design goal like "creating a game for logging emotions" initially. Instead, I set out with the obscure design goal of "designing something for people to better understand their emotions". It was after a series of brainstorming, testing, and iterations that I finally arrived at the design of "Bunny Mood". Final Design

Feed the bunny（Log emotions）

I introduced four types of bunny food in the final design: emo-ball, emo-soup, emo-salad, and emo-burger, catering to different users' logging habits and helping to analyze and understand emotions from various perspectives.

The emo-ball is the most convenient option for creating food. You can record your emotions by drawing expressive visuals and providing accompanying text descriptions.
 

Emo-soup enables the understanding of granularity and complexity. When preparing an emo-soup, you can assign a name to your current emotion and select the fundamental emotions that make up the mixed feeling.

In an emo-burger, you can upload media files such as sound, photos, and videos to record your emotions. This feature allows you to capture and preserve your current emotions more vividly.

Emo-salad encourages you to analyze your emotions from a non-judgmental perspective, in which you can name your emotions and choose relevant tags. The tag selection helps you explore the underlying causes of emotional challenges you may be facing, facilitating problem-solving and the development of effective strategies. This process aligning with techniques commonly used by therapists and social emotional learning educators to enhance emotional skills.

You may have noticed that some food items display a number. This represents the number of raw food materials. If you run out of a type of raw food materials, you are unable to make this food, like we are unable to make emo-burger in the following mockup. But we can purchase some emo-burger materials from the shop, using Bunny poops, the in-game currency. Emo-ball is an exception as it is the simplest food that doesn't require raw food materials to produce.

The inclusion of raw food materials serves two purposes:

Firstly, it accommodates different recording preferences of players. By unlocking different feeding panels through the purchasing of various raw food materials, players with specific recording habits won't feel redundant, allowing each player to choose the method that suits them best.

Secondly, raw food materials serve as consumable items. If there were no consumable items and players had already purchased all available items from the shop, collecting emo-chocolates would become pointless, disrupting the game loop. The inclusion of raw food materials overcomes this issue and maintains the game's flow.

In the current version, I've designed four types of bunny food, but the interface layout allows for the potential addition of more food varieties in future updates.

Your bunny will only go out to play if it has eaten the food for the day. The more food it eats, the greater the possibility of bringing back emotional fragments. If you forget to feed your bunny for more than three days, the bunny will go into a dormant state. You will need to feed it for three consecutive days to resume its activity but rest assured, it won't die. It would be too harsh if the bunny could die, and I want to ensure that players don't feel saddened in my game.
 
 

Purchase and place items to influence the bunny's activities


 
Besides raw food materials, players can utilize emo-chocolates to purchase items in the other three categories: articles, clothes, and furniture.
 
 

Players can purchase different clothes to dress up their bunnies. These clothes will influence the locations where the bunnies go. For example, if the bunny is dressed in a swimsuit, it may bring back an emotion fragment that occurred at the swimming pool. This design not only adds depth and enjoyment to the gameplay but also allows players to customize the appearance of their bunnies.

Articles will influence the bunny's activity depicted in the emotion fragment. For example, giving bunny a ticket may let it bring you a emotion fragment in with it is holding the ticket to take a train.

If Bunny is not at home, you can placed the articles on the room. When Bunny returns home, it will automatically pack the articles. If Bunny is at home, players also have the option to directly put the articles into Bunny's backpack.

Displaying all of the features on the main page might make players who prefer minimalist functions feel overwhelmed. To address this, extra features are integrated into different pieces of furniture. This not only allows players to customize the features but also enriches the shopping experience. It also facilitates the future addition of new features during game operation.

You can place a furniture on the room, and tap it to use its functions. The game incoporates AR so the room would be your real environment.


 Collect emotion fragments

When the bunny back home, it may bring you an emotion fragment. Collect the emotion fragments to compose a manga story that depicts a context-based emotion from the bunny.

Once you have completed a manga story, you can rate this emotion based on how often and strong you feel this, from 0~5.
 


 Social features

Due to the importance of social interaction that I discovered in the previous research, I incorporated several social features into the final design, and the mailbox icon at the bottom is the portal to most social-related functions.

If you and your friend trigger the “Feel link”, which means both of you rate the same manga with the same score on the same day, you will be notified and receive some emo-chocolate reward.


You will receive emotion fragments from your friends and you can also give yours to them.
 

You have the option to designate certain friends as "co-feeders". Co-feeders bunnies may visit your homes and consume the placed food. Similarly, if you forget to feed your bunny, your bunny may visit the co-feeder to find food.
